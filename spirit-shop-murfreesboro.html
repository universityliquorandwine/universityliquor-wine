Tennessee Whiskey Collection

Celebrating our state's legendary distilling heritage

Tennessee's Finest

Tennessee whiskey represents one of America's greatest spirits traditions. Distinct from bourbon by its unique charcoal mellowing process (known as the Lincoln County Process), Tennessee whiskey offers a smooth, refined character that has made it famous worldwide.

What Makes Tennessee Whiskey Special?

  • Lincoln County Process: Charcoal-mellowed before aging for extra smoothness
  • Local Heritage: Made right here in Tennessee with pride and tradition
  • Premium Quality: Carefully crafted using time-honored methods
  • Distinctive Flavor: Smoother than bourbon with subtle maple sweetness

Classic Cocktails

Timeless recipes to showcase our premium spirits

Old Fashioned

Bourbon or Rye Whiskey

Ingredients:
  • 2 oz Bourbon or Rye
  • 1 Sugar cube
  • 2-3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Orange peel

Method: Muddle sugar with bitters and a splash of water. Add whiskey and ice. Stir well. Garnish with orange peel.

Classic

Classic Martini

Gin or Vodka

Ingredients:
  • 2.5 oz Gin or Vodka
  • 0.5 oz Dry Vermouth
  • Lemon twist or olives

Method: Stir gin and vermouth with ice. Strain into chilled martini glass. Garnish with lemon twist or olives.

Elegant

Margarita

Tequila

Ingredients:
  • 2 oz Tequila (100% agave)
  • 1 oz Fresh lime juice
  • 0.5 oz Orange liqueur
  • Salt for rim

Method: Shake all ingredients with ice. Strain into salt-rimmed glass. Garnish with lime wheel.

Refreshing

Manhattan

Rye or Bourbon

Ingredients:
  • 2 oz Rye or Bourbon
  • 1 oz Sweet vermouth
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry

Method: Stir all ingredients with ice. Strain into chilled coupe glass. Garnish with cherry.

Sophisticated

Negroni

Gin

Ingredients:
  • 1 oz Gin
  • 1 oz Campari
  • 1 oz Sweet vermouth
  • Orange peel

Method: Stir all ingredients with ice. Strain into rocks glass over fresh ice. Garnish with orange peel.

Bitter & Bold

Moscow Mule

Vodka

Ingredients:
  • 2 oz Vodka
  • 0.5 oz Fresh lime juice
  • 4 oz Ginger beer
  • Lime wheel, mint

Method: Build in copper mug over ice. Add vodka and lime. Top with ginger beer. Stir gently. Garnish with lime and mint.

Spirits Education & Resources

Enhance your spirits knowledge with our expert guides

Major Whiskey Categories:
  • Bourbon: Made from at least 51% corn, aged in new charred oak barrels. Sweet with vanilla and caramel notes.
  • Tennessee Whiskey: Similar to bourbon but filtered through sugar maple charcoal (Lincoln County Process).
  • Rye Whiskey: Made from at least 51% rye grain. Spicier and drier than bourbon.
  • Scotch: Made in Scotland, typically from malted barley. Ranges from light and floral to rich and peaty.
  • Irish Whiskey: Triple-distilled for smoothness. Light and approachable with subtle fruit notes.
  • Japanese Whisky: Inspired by Scotch but with unique refinement and precision.
Understanding Alcohol Content:

ABV (Alcohol by Volume): The percentage of alcohol in the spirit. Most spirits are 40% ABV or higher.

Proof: In the US, proof is simply ABV multiplied by 2. So 40% ABV = 80 proof.

Common Proof Ranges:
  • 80 Proof (40% ABV): Standard for most vodka, gin, rum
  • 90-100 Proof: Higher-proof vodkas and whiskeys
  • 100+ Proof: Barrel-proof whiskeys, overproof rum
  • Navy Strength (114 Proof): Traditional high-proof gin

Tip: Higher proof doesn't necessarily mean better quality, but it can deliver more intense flavors.

The Aging Process:

Many premium spirits are aged in wooden barrels (usually oak), which imparts flavor, color, and character.

What Aging Adds:
  • Color: Clear spirits turn golden to dark amber
  • Flavor: Vanilla, caramel, spice, oak, and toffee notes
  • Smoothness: Harsh alcohol edges are mellowed
  • Complexity: Layers of flavor develop over time
Age Statements:
  • Whiskey: Common ages are 4, 8, 12, 15, 18, 21+ years
  • Rum: Often aged 5-15 years in Caribbean climates
  • Tequila: Blanco (unaged), Reposado (2-12 months), A√±ejo (1-3 years), Extra A√±ejo (3+ years)
  • Cognac: VS (2+ years), VSOP (4+ years), XO (10+ years)
The Proper Tasting Process:
  1. Choose the Right Glass: Use a tulip-shaped glass or Glencairn for whiskey. Coupe or rocks glass for others.
  2. Observe the Color: Hold up to light. Color indicates age and barrel type.
  3. Nose It: Take short sniffs with mouth slightly open. Don't stick nose deep in glass.
  4. Add Water (Optional): A few drops can open up flavors, especially in high-proof spirits.
  5. Small Sip: Let it coat your palate. Don't swallow immediately.
  6. Evaluate: Notice sweetness, spice, fruit, oak, and finish length.
Tasting Terms:
  • Nose: The aroma/smell of the spirit
  • Palate: The taste when in your mouth
  • Finish: Flavors that linger after swallowing
  • Legs: The way spirit runs down the glass (indicates viscosity)
Optimal Serving Temperatures:
  • Whiskey/Bourbon: Room temperature (60-65¬∞F) or with a large ice cube
  • Vodka/Gin: Chilled (kept in freezer or over ice)
  • Tequila: Room temperature for sipping, chilled for shots
  • Rum: Room temperature for aged, chilled for white rum
  • Cognac: Room temperature in a snifter
Storage Guidelines:
  • Store bottles upright (unlike wine)
  • Keep away from direct sunlight
  • Avoid temperature fluctuations
  • Keep caps/corks tight to prevent oxidation
  • Once opened, spirits last for years (not like wine)
  • Liqueurs with cream should be refrigerated after opening
